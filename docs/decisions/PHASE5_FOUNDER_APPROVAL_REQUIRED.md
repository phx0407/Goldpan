## From DEC000003 (Intake Evidence Ingestion Architecture) — APPROVED 2026-07-16

Approved as ruled during the 2026-07-16 Founder review session, which resolved six blocking decisions (originally numbered §13.1-§13.6 in the DEC000003 groundwork document) plus a preceding interpretive ruling on DEC000001 §5.7. **This approval covers policy and semantics only** — the additive `evidence.*` schema migration, the approve-RPC precondition, the Evidence Disposition mechanism's schema/API/audit implementation, the narrowly-scoped review-UI controls, and `commit_ingest` itself all remain unbuilt. The canonical Decision Record, `docs/decisions/DEC000003_CANONICAL_INTAKE_EVIDENCE_INGESTION_ARCHITECTURE.md`, was formalized 2026-07-18 from the Founder's 2026-07-16 rulings, recorded contemporaneously in `docs/decisions/DEC000003_INTAKE_EVIDENCE_SCHEMA_GAP_MAP.md` (the companion technical document, retained as the schema-gap and implementation-sequencing reference). Document propagation from this approval: `docs/GOLDPAN_COMMAND_REGISTRY_PHASE5.md` (§22 resolved block, CMD000008 entries in §17/§20, Priority 2/5 in §24) and `docs/GOLDPAN_MASTER_OS_BLUEPRINT.md` (`approved` row of the Intake Packet lifecycle table) have been updated to reference DEC000003.

19. **Approved — DEC000001 §5.7 is not amended.** The "durable write succeeds" criterion for `approved → ingested` holds its strict reading: every approved, ingestion-bound evidence element must be durably and faithfully persisted, or the packet stays `approved` — no category-level exception, no skip-and-log path, no `partially_ingested` state.
20. **Approved — the Evidence Disposition mechanism.** Rejected silently dropping unclassifiable evidence and rejected forcing every disclosure into a fixed classification with no escape path. Captured evidence is never silently removed; a reviewer may record an extensible, audited disposition (actor, authority basis, timestamp, required non-blank reason) that excludes an item from the ingestion-bound set without deleting it from `packet_data` or audit history. The disposition-value model must accept future values without a structural rework — not hardcoded to a single value.
21. **Approved — the approved-packet correction invariant, with `intake.packet.reopen` still explicitly undefined.** Any change to approved `packet_data` requires the governed `intake.packet.reopen` workflow (non-blank reason, authorized actor, distinct audit event, full re-review). An Evidence Disposition backfill on an already-approved packet does not require reopening, since it never touches `packet_data`. `reopen` (CMD000010) itself remains reserved and undefined, per DEC000001 line 38 and §5.9 — this decision does not design it, and does not reopen or amend DEC000001.
22. **Approved — the two existing-packet remediation paths.** Path b1 (Evidence Disposition backfill, no `packet_data` mutation, no reopen required) is approved for immediate use once built. Path b2 (direct administrative edit to an approved packet's `packet_data` without reopening) is **not approved** — any such remediation must go through `intake.packet.reopen` once that command exists.
23. **Approved — `commit_ingest`'s precondition scope and failure contract.** The completeness precondition evaluates only evidence entries that actually exist in `packet_data` (a dish with zero allergen-disclosure entries imposes no requirement; a packet with none anywhere passes automatically). On failure, `commit_ingest` fails the whole call with SQLSTATE `GP422` (reusing the existing four-code taxonomy — `GP403`/`GP404`/`GP409`/`GP422` — established across migrations 017-019, not a newly minted code), leaves `packet_status` unchanged, and returns a structured payload identifying `evidence_type`, `missing_fields`, dish/restaurant context, `blocked_count`, and `corrective_action_required`.
24. **Approved — calorie evidence is optional and never inferred.** All calorie-related columns on `evidence.dishes` (including the new defensive `calorie_raw_fragment jsonb` fidelity copy) must remain nullable; absence of calorie evidence must never block approval or ingestion; `commit_ingest` must never infer, estimate, or fabricate a calorie value; restaurant-published calorie data must stay a distinct record from any future GoldPan-estimated calorie model.
