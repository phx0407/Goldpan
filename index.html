The Opportunity

Some Restaurants Tell You Everything. We Help You Find Them.

There are restaurants that are transparent. Ones that tell you exactly what is in your food, how it is prepared, and what is in their food. They deserve to be found.

Goldpan scores every dish on ingredient transparency so diners who want to know what is in their food can find the restaurants that are already being transparent.

Transparent Restaurants Win
Restaurants that disclose their ingredients get recognized, discovered, and trusted by the diners who care most.
Diners Get Confidence
Filter by allergen or dietary need and instantly see which dishes are clear and how confident you can be in that answer.
Trust Goes Both Ways
The score reflects how clearly and thoroughly a restaurant communicates what is in their food. Any restaurant, any cuisine, any price point can score highly by communicating honestly.

Why It Matters

The restaurants diners trust are the ones they find first.
01
Stand Out Instantly
In a city full of options, transparency is a differentiator. A Goldpan score tells diners your restaurant is worth choosing before they ever see a review.
02
Stop Losing Guests Before They Arrive
Diners with dietary needs do their research first. If they cannot find clear ingredient information, they move on. They never walk through the door.
03
The Guest Who Finds You Stays
Better communication attracts better-fit customers. Customers who find exactly what they are looking for return more and bring people with them.

How It Works

How We Review
Every Dish

01
We Map the Menu
Every dish is broken down ingredient by ingredient. Protein, base, sauces, seasonings. Exactly as described. No inference. No guessing.
02
We Assess the Clarity
Each dish receives a transparency level — Building, Moderate, or High — based on how clearly a restaurant communicates across four dimensions: ingredients, sauces, allergens, and preparation.
03
You See What Is Disclosed
Filter by allergen or dietary need and instantly see which dishes are clear, which are unknown, and what the restaurant has shared.
